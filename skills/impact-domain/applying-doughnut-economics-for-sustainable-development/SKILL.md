# Applying Doughnut Economics for Sustainable Development

## Introduction
This skill focuses on utilizing the Doughnut Economics framework to design and implement policies that achieve a balance between human prosperity and environmental sustainability. It involves considering both social foundations and planetary boundaries to ensure a 'safe and just space' for humanity.

## Key Concepts

### Doughnut Economics Framework
- **Inner Boundary (Social Foundation)**: Ensures that human rights and basic needs are met.
- **Outer Boundary (Environmental Ceiling)**: Maintains planetary boundaries to prevent ecological degradation.
- **Doughnut Area**: Represents the safe and just space for sustainable and inclusive development.

## Principles for Application

### Equity in Resource Use
- Emphasize fair distribution of resources.
- Focus on reducing the consumption of the wealthiest while meeting basic needs for all.

### Efficiency in Resource Transformation
- Maximize resource efficiency.
- Aim to meet human needs without exceeding planetary limits.

### Integration of Boundaries
- Synchronize social and environmental policies to support sustainability.

## Policy Guidelines

### Poverty Eradication
- Aim to achieve the social foundation for all without exceeding environmental boundaries.
- Ensure that policy actions do not inadvertently stress planetary limits.
- **Example**: Providing electricity to those without could increase CO2 emissions by less than 1%.

### Resource Consumption Reduction by the Wealthy
- Address excessive consumption and production patterns among the wealthiest 10%.

## Decision Rules

### Cross-boundary Consideration
- Avoid policies that force people below the social foundation in the pursuit of environmental targets.
- **Example**: Carbon markets need careful design to prevent land grabs that displace marginalized communities.

## Challenges

### Distribution Dynamics
- Recognize inequities and inefficiencies leading to unsustainable practices.

### Political Feasibility
- Understand complexities in international resource management and fairness.
