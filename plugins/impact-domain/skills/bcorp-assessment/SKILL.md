# B Corp Certification Framework Implementation

## Overview

This skill outlines how to implement the B Corp Certification framework by fulfilling criteria across seven key impact areas: governance, climate action, inclusivity, government affairs, fair work, human rights, and environmental stewardship. It emphasizes continuous improvement, third-party verification, and transparency in social and environmental performance.

## Certification Framework

### Foundation Requirements
- **Legal Accountability**: Ensure legal structures support commitment to purpose beyond profit.
- **Transparency**: Maintain open communication on performance metrics.
- **Commitment to Improvement**: Exhibit dedication to progressive enhancement in all impact areas.

## Impact Area Requirements

### 1. Purpose and Stakeholder Governance (PSG)
- Define and embed a mission-focused purpose in governance.
- Engage stakeholders in decision-making processes.
- Implement board oversight and publish annual progress reports.

### 2. Climate Action (CA)
- Conduct a greenhouse gas (GHG) inventory and set science-based targets.
- Develop and disclose transition plans, verify reductions, and engage suppliers.

### 3. Justice, Equity, Diversity & Inclusion (JEDI)
- Collect and disclose diversity data, implement leadership diversity targets.
- Conduct pay equity analysis and enforce anti-harassment policies.

### 4. Government Affairs and Collective Action (GACA)
- Align public policy positions with company purpose and disclose lobbying activities.
- Engage in sustainability initiatives that reflect corporate values.

### 5. Fair Work (FW)
- Ensure payment of living wages, enforce workplace safety, and provide competitive benefits.
- Maintain formal grievance mechanisms and assess supply chain labor risks.

### 6. Human Rights (HR)
- Establish public human rights policies and conduct due diligence on value chain risks.
- Engage with communities, offering remedy mechanisms and securing third-party verifications.

### 7. Environmental Stewardship and Circularity (ESC)
- Track and disclose metrics, set reduction targets, and design for circularity.

## Scoring and Assessment
- Achieve a minimum score of 80/200 points, ensuring no single topic offsets another's importance.

## Practical Assessment Process
1. Review foundation requirements.
2. Conduct self-assessment and gap analysis.
3. Prioritize and implement improvements.
4. Submit assessment for third-party verification.
5. Publish impact reports and set future targets.
